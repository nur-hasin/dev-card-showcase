📖 Blockchain Lab Manual

What is this?

This is a live, mathematical simulation of a Distributed Ledger (Blockchain) powered by a custom-built SHA-256 cryptographic hashing engine written entirely in Vanilla JavaScript.

How the Tech Works:

  • Hashing: Every block compresses its data into a unique 64-character string. If even one letter of data changes, the entire hash changes.
  • The Chain: Every new block permanently records the hash of the previous block. If past data is tampered with, the link breaks!
  • Proof of Work: To secure a block, the computer must "Mine" it by guessing a random number (the Nonce) repeatedly until the resulting hash starts with a specific number of zeros (the Difficulty).

How to Use the Simulator:

  1. Create Data: Enter a Sender, Receiver, and Amount in the New Transaction card.
  2. Pool It: Click "Add to Pool". The transaction is now pending, waiting to be secured in a block.
  3. Mine the Block: Click "Mine Block". Watch the Live Visualizer rapidly guess thousands of nonces in real-time until it solves the cryptographic puzzle!
  4. Inspect: Look at the newly added block on the right to see its Timestamp, Nonce, and cryptographic links.
